EU Drone Regulation (2019/945)

Since [date], all drones sold in the EU must comply with Delegated Regulation (EU) 2019/945. This regulation establishes technical requirements for drones and classifies them into categories.

Drone Classes Explained

Class Description Typical Use
C0 Toy drones, <250g, max speed 19m/s, no audio recording Mini whoops, tiny whoops, indoor flyers
C1 <900g, max speed 19m/s, geo-awareness, direct remote identification Small cinewhoops, micro freestyle (3-4")
C2 <4kg, low-speed mode, geo-awareness, remote ID 5" freestyle, 5" racing, most hobby drones
C3 <25kg, same as C2 but heavier Long-range 7-10", heavy lift
C4 Non-autonomous, <25kg, no automatic control Specialist builds, model aircraft

Each product page clearly displays the class of the drone. You can also find the class on the product packaging and in the user manual.
