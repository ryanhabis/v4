# 📁 Legal Folder Contents - FPV Build Lab (Ireland)

This document explains every file in the `/legal/` folder of your website. These pages ensure your business is fully compliant with Irish and EU law.

---

## 📂 Folder Structure

```
/legal/
│
├── index.html          # Legal Hub - Main landing page for all policies
├── imprint.html        # Business Information (Impressum)
├── privacy.html        # GDPR Privacy Policy
├── terms.html          # Terms & Conditions of Sale
├── returns.html        # Returns & Cooling-Off Policy
├── shipping.html       # Dangerous Goods (LiPo Battery) Shipping Policy
├── weee.html           # WEEE Compliance & Recycling Information
├── compliance.html     # EU Compliance & CE Certification
├── cookie-policy.html  # Cookie Policy
└── liability.html      # Liability Disclaimer
```

---

## 📄 File-by-File Explanation

### 1. `index.html` - Legal Hub
**Purpose:** Central dashboard linking to all legal pages.

**Why you need it:**
- Customers can find all policies in one place
- Professional appearance
- Required for GDPR transparency

**Key sections:**
- Grid of all legal pages with descriptions
- Compliance badges
- Quick contact for legal enquiries

---

### 2. `imprint.html` - Business Information (Impressum)
**Purpose:** Displays your legal business identity as required by Irish law.

**Why you need it:**
- **Required by Irish Companies Act 2014** - All businesses selling online must display:
  - Business name and legal structure
  - Registered address
  - Contact information
  - VAT number
  - Company registration number (if applicable)

**Key sections:**
- Business name, address, contact
- VAT number, CRO number
- Regulatory compliance statements
- Insurance details
- Dispute resolution information

---

### 3. `privacy.html` - GDPR Privacy Policy
**Purpose:** Explains how you collect, use, and protect customer data.

**Why you need it:**
- **Required by GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)** - Fines up to €20 million or 4% of global turnover for non-compliance
- Must be easily accessible to all customers
- Must explain:
  - What data you collect
  - Why you collect it
  - Legal basis (GDPR Article 6)
  - How long you keep it
  - Who you share it with
  - Customer rights

**Key sections:**
- Data collection table
- Cookie explanation
- Third-party processors (Stripe, PayPal, couriers)
- Your rights under GDPR
- How to complain to Data Protection Commission

---

### 4. `terms.html` - Terms & Conditions of Sale
**Purpose:** The legal contract between you and your customers.

**Why you need it:**
- **Required by EU Consumer Rights Directive**
- Protects you from disputes
- Sets clear rules for:
  - Order process
  - Pricing
  - Shipping
  - Returns
  - Warranty
  - Liability

**Key sections:**
- Product category definitions (RTF kits, components, custom builds)
- Custom build legal structure (important for liability)
- Dangerous goods terms
- Warranty terms
- Liability limitations
- Governing law (Irish law)

---

### 5. `returns.html` - Returns & Cooling-Off Policy
**Purpose:** Explains customers' 14-day right to cancel and return process.

**Why you need it:**
- **Required by EU Consumer Rights Directive 2011/83/EU**
- Customers have unconditional right to cancel within 14 days
- Must explain:
  - How to cancel
  - Who pays return shipping
  - When refunds are issued
  - Exceptions (digital goods, batteries)

**Key sections:**
- Quick reference table by product type
- Step-by-step return process
- Condition requirements
- Exceptions (LiPo batteries, digital goods)
- Model withdrawal form template

---

### 6. `shipping.html` - Dangerous Goods Shipping Policy
**Purpose:** Explains how LiPo batteries are shipped and safety requirements.

**Why you need it:**
- **Required by IATA Dangerous Goods Regulations & ADR**
- LiPo batteries are Class 9 Dangerous Goods
- Shipping illegally can result in:
  - Fines up to €5,000 per incident
  - Criminal charges
  - Loss of shipping privileges

**Key sections:**
- Why LiPos are dangerous goods
- Our shipping process (packaging, labeling, documentation)
- DG shipping costs and surcharges
- Prohibited destinations
- Your responsibilities as buyer
- Battery safety information
- Emergency procedures

---

### 7. `weee.html` - WEEE Compliance
**Purpose:** Explains electrical waste recycling obligations.

**Why you need it:**
- **Required by WEEE Directive (SI 149 of 2014)**
- All sellers of electrical equipment must:
  - Register with WEEE Ireland
  - Provide take-back service
  - Inform customers about recycling

**Key sections:**
- Our WEEE registration number
- Why recycling matters
- Products covered
- Your responsibilities
- Free take-back service
- Battery recycling information
- Environmental benefits

---

### 8. `compliance.html` - EU Compliance & CE Certification
**Purpose:** Demonstrates that your products meet EU regulations.

**Why you need it:**
- **Required by EU Drone Regulation 2019/945**
- All drones sold must:
  - Be CE marked
  - Have class identification (C0-C4)
  - Have EU Declaration of Conformity
- Builds trust with customers and authorities

**Key sections:**
- Our commitment to compliance
- Drone classes explained (C0-C4)
- CE marking requirements
- EU Declaration of Conformity
- Radio Equipment Directive (RED)
- Your responsibilities as operator
- How to request compliance documents

---

### 9. `cookie-policy.html` - Cookie Policy
**Purpose:** Explains how cookies are used and obtains consent.

**Why you need it:**
- **Required by ePrivacy Directive & GDPR**
- Must explain:
  - What cookies are
  - Which cookies you use
  - Why you use them
  - How to manage them
- Must obtain consent before setting non-essential cookies

**Key sections:**
- What are cookies
- Types of cookies we use (essential, analytics, affiliate)
- Cookie table with names and purposes
- Your consent options
- How to manage cookies in browser
- Third-party cookies

---

### 10. `liability.html` - Liability Disclaimer
**Purpose:** Limits your legal liability for accidents and misuse.

**Why you need it:**
- **Critical for FPV business** - Drones can cause serious injury
- Warns customers of inherent risks
- Limits your exposure to lawsuits
- Required by Product Liability Directive (partial defence)

**Key sections:**
- Assumption of risk
- Operator responsibility
- No liability for damages
- Product use warnings
- Legal compliance reminder
- Indemnification clause
- Acknowledgment statement

## ✅ Why You Need ALL These Pages

| Risk | Consequence if Missing |
|------|------------------------|
| No imprint | Fine from CRO, customers can't find you |
| No privacy policy | GDPR fine up to €20 million |
| No terms | Disputes harder to win, unclear rules |
| No returns policy | Must accept returns indefinitely |
| No DG policy | Illegal shipping, fines, criminal charges |
| No WEEE | Fine from EPA, environmental non-compliance |
| No compliance docs | Products may be seized by IAA/ComReg |
| No cookie policy | GDPR violation, consent issues |
| No liability disclaimer | You're personally liable for accidents |
